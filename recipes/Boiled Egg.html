Boiled Egg or Ajitsuke Tamago

Description

"Indulge in the culinary magic of Ajitsuke Tamago, a tantalizing Japanese treat that's as enchanting as a scene from your favorite anime! Picture perfectly boiled eggs, their golden yolks wrapped in a silky embrace. These eggs take a flavorful plunge into a marinade concoction of soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and a hint of sake, creating a symphony of taste that dances on your taste buds."

Ingredients

note: serving size is 4 eggs
  • 4 eggs
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of sake (optional)
  • water (for boiling)

Instructions

  1. Boil the eggs for 7-9 minutes, depending on your desired yolk consistency.
  2. Transfer boiled eggs to ice water to cool and peel.
  3. In a saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, sugar, and sake.
  4. Heat until sugar dissolves, then let it cool.
  5. Place peeled eggs in a container.
  6. Pour the cooled marinade over the eggs, ensuring they are fully submerged.
  7. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
